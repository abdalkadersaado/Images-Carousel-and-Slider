Birds

Birds are vertebrate animals belonging to the class Aves, characterized by feathers, a beak with no teeth, and laying hard-shelled eggs. They are found in a wide range of habitats worldwide and are known for their ability to fly (although not all birds can fly).

Elephants

Elephants are large mammals belonging to the family Elephantidae, which includes three species: the African bush elephant, the African forest elephant, and the Asian elephant. They are known for their distinct features such as their large ears, long trunks, tusks, and significant size.

Mountains

Mountains are prominent landforms that rise significantly above the surrounding terrain, usually characterized by steep slopes and significant height differences. They are formed through various geological processes and can be found on every continent

Seas

Seas are large bodies of saltwater that are smaller than oceans and are usually partially enclosed by land. They are integral parts of the Earth's hydrosphere and play a crucial role in climate regulation, biodiversity, and human activities.

Gazelle

A gazelle is a small to medium-sized antelope characterized by its slender build, long legs, and often graceful movements.
